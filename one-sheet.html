Programming Concept Overview
Jazz Ting Radio × World Cup 2026 is a live DJ broadcast series built around every FIFA match day in Atlanta. Each event features a vinyl-first DJ set curated around the countries playing that day — drawing from traditional music, diaspora sounds, and contemporary club culture. Broadcasts are filmed cinematically, streamed live to YouTube, and clipped for social. The format runs pre-game through post-game: downtempo jazz and soul building to Amapiano and Afro-house at match time, closing with cross-cultural mashups. This is not a watch party — it's a live cultural broadcast with the stadium as backdrop.
Uniquely Connected to Atlanta Culture
Jazz Ting Radio has broadcast live, all-vinyl DJ sets from a studio with direct line-of-sight to Mercedes-Benz Stadium for six years. Our sound reflects Atlanta's DNA — a city built on Black musical innovation, global migration, and cultural collision. We blend Amapiano, Jazz House, Hip-hop, and Rare Groove the same way Atlanta blends trap, gospel, and R&B: without apology. Dell Harris, founder and host, has spent years building a broadcast practice rooted in the Mitchell Street corridor — Atlanta's original cultural spine. This work is not inspired by Atlanta. It lives inside it.
Translating Atlanta Culture to a Global Audience
Jazz Ting Radio speaks the language every World Cup visitor already understands: music. Our match-day broadcasts are built around the countries competing — curating Brazilian samba into house, Nigerian Afrobeats into Amapiano, French jazz into club rhythms — and threading Atlanta's sound through every set. Streamed live to YouTube and clipped for social, our content travels globally the moment it airs. International visitors won't just hear Atlanta — they'll hear their own culture reflected through it. That exchange is how Jazz Ting Radio introduces the world to Atlanta: through groove, not geography.
Community Impact
Jazz Ting Radio serves Atlanta's creative community — DJs, producers, vinyl collectors, and music lovers who rarely see their tastes reflected in major event programming. Participation in ATL Culture House would validate this work as essential to the World Cup narrative, creating a platform for underrepresented sound in a historically mainstream event. It creates paid opportunities for local talent, amplifies Atlanta's independent music scene to a global audience, and builds the foundation for Jazz Ting Café — a permanent cultural venue rooted in the same community we've served for six years.
